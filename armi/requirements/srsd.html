1. Software Requirement Specification Document (SRSD)¶

1.1. Functional Requirements¶

Requirement: ARMI shall be able to represent a user-specified reactor. REQ_REACTOR
status: implemented, needs more tests
links incoming: IMPL_REACTOR_0, TEST_REACTOR_0, TEST_REACTOR_1, TEST_REACTOR_2
child needs: REQ_REACTOR_HIERARCHY, REQ_REACTOR_THERMAL_EXPANSION, REQ_REACTOR_SHAPES, REQ_REACTOR_MESH, REQ_REACTOR_4, REQ_REACTOR_5, REQ_REACTOR_6, REQ_REACTOR_7

Given user input describing a reactor system, ARMI shall construct with equivalent fidelity a software model of the reactor. In particular, ARMI shall appropriately represent the shape, arrangement, connectivity, dimensions, materials (including thermo-mechanical properties), isotopic composition, and temperatures of the reactor.

Requirement: ARMI shall represent the reactor hierarchically. REQ_REACTOR_HIERARCHY
status: completed
links incoming: IMPL_REACTOR_HIERARCHY_0, TEST_REACTOR_HIERARCHY_0
parent needs: REQ_REACTOR

To maintain consistency with the physical reactor being modeled, ARMI shall maintain a hierarchical definition of its components. For example, all the fuel pins in a single fuel assembly in a solid-fuel reactor shall be collected such that they can be queried or modified as a unit as well as individuals.
Requirement: ARMI shall automatically handle thermal expansion. REQ_REACTOR_THERMAL_EXPANSION
status: completed
links incoming: TEST_REACTOR_THERMAL_EXPANSION_0, TEST_REACTOR_THERMAL_EXPANSION_1, TEST_REACTOR_THERMAL_EXPANSION_2, TEST_REACTOR_THERMAL_EXPANSION_3, TEST_REACTOR_THERMAL_EXPANSION_4, TEST_REACTOR_THERMAL_EXPANSION_5, TEST_REACTOR_THERMAL_EXPANSION_6, TEST_REACTOR_THERMAL_EXPANSION_7, TEST_REACTOR_THERMAL_EXPANSION_8, TEST_REACTOR_THERMAL_EXPANSION_9
parent needs: REQ_REACTOR

ARMI shall automatically compute and applied thermal expansion and contraction of materials.
Requirement: ARMI shall support a reasonable set of basic shapes. REQ_REACTOR_SHAPES
status: implemented, needs more tests
links incoming: IMPL_REACTOR_SHAPES_0, IMPL_REACTOR_SHAPES_1, IMPL_REACTOR_SHAPES_2
parent needs: REQ_REACTOR

ARMI shall support the following basic shapes: Hexagonal prism (ducts in fast reactors), rectangular prism (ducts in thermal reactors), cylindrical prism (fuel pins, cladding, etc.), and helix (wire wrap).
Requirement: ARMI shall support a number of structured mesh options. REQ_REACTOR_MESH
status: completed
links incoming: IMPL_REACTOR_MESH_0, IMPL_REACTOR_MESH_1, IMPL_REACTOR_MESH_2, IMPL_REACTOR_MESH_3
parent needs: REQ_REACTOR

ARMI shall support regular, repeating meshes in hexagonal, radial-zeta-theta (RZT), and Cartesian structures.
Requirement: ARMI shall support the specification of symmetry options and boundary conditions. REQ_REACTOR_4
status: implemented, need impl/test
parent needs: REQ_REACTOR

ARMI shall support symmetric models including 1/4, 1/8 core models for Cartesian meshes and 1/3 and full core for Hex meshes. For Cartesian 1/8 core symmetry, the core axial symmetry plane (midplane) will be located at the top of the reactor.
Requirement: ARMI shall check for basic correctness. REQ_REACTOR_5
status: implemented, need impl/test
parent needs: REQ_REACTOR

ARMI shall check its input for certain obvious errors including unphysical densities and proper fit.
Requirement: ARMI shall allow for the definition of limited one-dimensional translation paths. REQ_REACTOR_6
status: implemented, need impl/test
parent needs: REQ_REACTOR

ARMI shall allow the user specification of translation pathways for certain objects to follow, to support moving control mechanisms.
Requirement: ARMI shall allow the definition of fuel management operations (i.e. shuffling) REQ_REACTOR_7
status: implemented, need impl/test
parent needs: REQ_REACTOR

ARMI shall allow for the modeling of a reactor over multiple cycles.
Requirement: ARMI shall represent and reflect the evolving state of a reactor. REQ_1
status: implemented, needs test

The state shale be made available to users and modules, which may in turn modify the state (e.g. for analysis or based on the results of a physical calculation). ARMI shall fully define how all aspects of state may be accessed and modified and shall reflect any new state after it is applied.

State shall be represented as evolving eitehr through time (i.e. in a typical cycle- by-cycle analysis) or through a series of control configurations.
